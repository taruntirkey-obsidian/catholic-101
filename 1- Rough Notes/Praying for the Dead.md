To understand why, we need to understand Purgatory.
Read Catechism of Catholic Church **1030-1032**

Purgatory is the final purification of souls before entering heaven.
It is different than hell.
They're also part of the church.
Church teaches that souls are immediately judged after death.

The standard that Jesus gives is to be perfect as God is perfect (**Matthew 5:48**). St. Paul also stated in **Hebrews 12:14** that without holiness, we can not see God. It is difficult to imagine someone perfect to go to heaven without needing purification. In **Leviticus 19:2** God commanded Israel to be holy for He is Holy, even St. Peter emphasized this in his letter (**1 Peter 1:16**).

Bible does teach about Purgatory in **1 Corinthians 3:10-15**. St. Paul talks about a purifying fire that saves.

Jesus taught purgatory in simplified words in His parable of faithful servant **Luke 12:42-48**. The faithful servant is accepted in heaven, second is sent to hell (eternallly damned), but third and fourth get beatings and not thrown out with the disobedient or cut in pieces. If heaven and hell are the only result after physical death, the parable should have ended with the unfaithful servant being thrown out or cut in pieces. But there's definitely more to it. Beatings and not eternal death definitely mean something. Why would you beat? To correct and discipline. In this case, to perfect the souls.

There are other hints of Purgatory in teachings of Jesus, like until the debt is paid, one can not leave prison **Matthew 5:25-26**. Here, the prison is said to be a place from where a soul can leave once its debts are paid. This can not be hell as once the soul is judged to be separated from God, they stay in the same way. Hell is separation from God. We can say if a person dies in grace and friendship of God, they can hope to see heaven as St. Paul explains in **1 Corinthians 15:12-19**, pay attention to verse **19** specifically as St. Paul can be paraphrased as our hope can not be limited to this life.

Again, in **Matthew 12:31-32** while speaking about the sin against the Holy Spirit, Jesus tells an individual will not be forgiven for this sin in this age or the age to come. Jesus is speaking about this life and the life after death. God can not forgive those who keep themselves out of God's forgiving love. So, in other words, if those are in the state of grace and dies in friendship with God, they can hope for eternal life as their sins will be purged to attain perfection for entering heaven.

*In Matthew 12:31-32, Jesus speaks about the sin against the Holy Spirit, stating that it will not be forgiven in this age or the age to come.*
*What is this age, and the age to come? Jesus is talking about this life and the next. Now, just pause for a second. Doesn't this mean God's mercy extends to the afterlife as well sins against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven but other sins can. Isn't it awesome. We can strive to attain perfection but without God's intervention it would be impossible but with God it is possible (Matthew 19:26). This passage highlights the gravity of rejecting God's forgiveness. Essentially, Jesus emphasizes that those who persist in rejecting God's love and forgiveness will not experience it. Conversely, individuals who die in a state of grace, having maintained a relationship with God, can hope for eternal life. Through purification, their sins will be purged, allowing them to enter heaven in a state of perfection.*

Since nothing unclean can enter heaven (**Revelation 21:27**), souls are made perfect before entering heaven. (**Hebrews 12:23**)

Those going through the final purification are called church penitent. Their only suffering is that they don't know when they'll receive their reward. Now, if we know someone is suffering, isn't it a Christian thing to pray for them? Just like we are taught to pray of each other. Praying for those in Purgatory is an act derived from the love of a neighbor. Church considers them as fellow Christians. 

Church victorious (those in heaven), Church penitent (those in Purgatory), and Church militant (alive on earth) together with God in center is the whole church. Praying for each other, for those in need of our prayers, is only natural for a Christian.
